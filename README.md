### Hi, I'm Krishna 👋

I'm a cybersecurity engineer based in **Munich, Germany** — originally from Andhra Pradesh, India, via Mumbai and Stockholm.

I work in Blue Team and SOC operations. The short version: every second, a corporate network generates thousands of log events. My job is finding the three that actually matter.

Currently at **epay (Euronet)** — one of Europe's major payment networks. Keeping transactions safe at scale.

---

### A little about how I think

Six years in this field taught me one thing above all else: **most security failures aren't technical. They're human.** That's why I deliberately studied User Experience Design (MSc, Jönköping University, Sweden) after my cybersecurity MTech. Understanding how people interact with systems makes you a far better engineer.

I find the cat-and-mouse nature of threat intelligence genuinely fascinating. Feeds go stale. Attackers change tactics. You're always updating your mental model. There's no "done" — and I find that more honest than most fields.

---

### Things I've built and done

**At Aqilea, Stockholm** — ran the full Blue Team stack for a major Swedish telecom. Configured Cortex XSOAR's multi-tenancy model, wrote SOAR playbooks that cut incident resolution time by 30%, tuned ArcSight log pipelines, migrated to Fluent Bit to get the same capability at lower cost, ran a HAProxy proof-of-concept that replaced expensive enterprise load balancing. Kept 99.9% uptime throughout.

**At Reliance Industries, Mumbai** — built a Threat Intelligence Platform from scratch using open-source tooling and TAXII feeds. Built an automated penetration testing system (SQL injection, XSS, SSL vulnerabilities, firewall alertness). Built a CTF platform the company used for security hiring — because real skills show up better in a live challenge than on a resume. Ran company-wide adversarial phishing campaigns with a custom scoring system.

**Forensics research** — spent time at Kalasalingam University researching SIM card cloning. Recovered artefacts from cloned SIMs using imaging methods, benchmarked forensic tools against each other.
